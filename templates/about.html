How Player Valuability Ratings Work

Player Valuability Ratings are composed of many different factors of a player's games that can accurately predicate their impact on the court and how valuable they are to their team.

The first metric calculated is the players' averages over their teams most recent 5 games (or less depending on team/player). After that, the following is considered:

The main idea is to focus on players' statistics as a percentage, rather than the number. Most of the big 8 stats are converted to percentages of their team stats.

Additionally, for special statistics like turnovers, blocks, steals, where more of them means less inherent "luck" is involved are applied to an exponential function.

True shooting is also considered to determine this statistic.
